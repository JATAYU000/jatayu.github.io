How Did I Hugo?

Sure, here’s your paragraph with minimal grammar corrections:

Creating a Hugo server was easy. I followed the website’s procedure. Adding a post was straightforward too. For the themes part, I scrolled through the Hugo themes page but didn’t find what I was looking for. Then, I found a simple yet pretty theme, Hugo-ink, and git-cloned its theme repository. I made all the changes to suit my needs, like fonts, colors, icons, etc. Hosting was a bit challenging because I initially tried a workflow method from the Hugo Git page, and it didn’t work. After a few unsuccessful attempts, I paused the task. Last Sunday, I watched a YouTube tutorial on creating a Hugo blog and successfully hosted the server. I created a blog repo with my Hugo contents, themes, TOML files, etc., and another repo, jatayu.github.io, hosted the site with the blog repo as a submodule. I’m happy with how it turned out and love the look.
